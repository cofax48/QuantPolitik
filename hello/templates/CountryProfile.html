People Power

In this section 3 key areas are evaluated. How powerful a country is, how big it is geographically, and how many citizens it has.
The idea is quite simple, the bigger a country is the more impotant it is, and the greater the nessescity in maintaining close ties. This is not to say that small countries are unimportant, but rather that all things being equal-the larger the country the greater the desire for interaction.

Financial Power

In this area, major developmental indicators are evaluated. The logic is that the wealthier and more developed a country is, the mor eit will hav to offer vis-a-vis trade and as an attractive investment destination. By including developmental clout, this area serves as either a counterpoint or force multiplier to the previous area of size. Since a small rich country can balance out a big poor country (Singapore vs. DRC), but a big rich country (Germany) vastly outperforms a small poor one (Burundi).
Therefore, the components under consideration are:
GDP in Billions at PPP, 5 Year GDP Growth Rate in Percentage, GDP per Capita in PPP, Unemployment in Percent, Public Debt as Percentage of GDP, Human Development Index

Global Commonalities

This section evaluates a couple disparate domains:
Same language, Opinion of the US, Free Trade Agreements, Shared Currency.
The logic here is that countries with the same lanaguage are more likely to engage one another. If they share the same currency, their foturnes are closely tied, and if they have a favorable approval of another, then the possibilities of cooperation increase even further.
