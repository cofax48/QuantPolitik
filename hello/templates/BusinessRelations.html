Business Relations

The guiding principle behind business relations is that corporate interests hold huge sways over a country’s bilateral relationship and offer fertile ground for entanglement. If a country is easy to invest in (Canada) Country A’s business are going to be more likely to increase their investment-consequently, business managers will travel back and forth, FDI will flow in and out, and finished products will travel en mass between both places. These effects will also constrain political maneuvering in both capitals, lest adverse political solutions take an unnecessary toll on corporate bottom lines. Essentially if money, people, and product flow freely between both places, leaders of both countries will interact often (to try and get good deals), build relationships (through interacting) and will be loathe jeopardizing fruitful business ties through aggression or conflict. Ergo, the better the climate for business, the more fertile the ground will be for growth in their relationships.

Business Climate

The first of Business Relations is to evaluate the climate of starting a business, the concept being that the easier it is to start a business, the more likely people will do so, thus increasing ties between both nations. Ergo, the greater the likelihood of starting a new business, the greater potential trade will be, the more frequent, leaders will meet, and the lower the likelihood that war will break out-based on the "Capitalist Peace Theory".
Notable examples of poor climates, are the Indian license Raj, the "Brazil Cost", Consequently, data that evaluates the ease of starting a business is evaluated:
Starting a Business, Dealing with Construction Permits, Getting Electricity, Registering Property, Registering Property, Getting Credit, Resolving Insolvency

Corporate Climate

In this area, the ease of operation for corporations are evaluated. The logic being that the easier it is for multinaionals and other corporations to operate, the more likely they are to do so. Consequently, rich and powerful corporate titans will take an interest in stable relations proliferating between both countries-lest their bottom lines are negatively impacted.
Therefore, the components under consideration are:
Paying Taxes, Trading Across Border, Enforcing Contracts, Business Freedom, Corporate Tax Rate Percentage

Financial Climate

In this area, the ease of moving mony in and out of the country is evalauted, and how wll investors in the country are protected. The general idea being that with fewer barries to capital flows, more money will move in country and thus spur development. If it's easy to move capital in, and investors are protected, even more invstment is likely to flow, consequently bolstering business ties.
Therefore, the components under consideration are:
Protecting Minority Investors, Monetary Freedom, Fiscal Freedom, Investment Freedom, Financial Freedom, Remittances
