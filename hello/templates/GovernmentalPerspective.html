Governmental Perspective

Here is where the “freedom credentials” of each country are evaluated. Political freedom, press freedom, freedom from corruption and other areas are evaluated. Freedom isn’t held in high regard because America is “free,” rather the concept is that free societies are less likely to do things that are going to upset the other country. Countries with respect for human right create fewer problems for their citizens, citizens of other countries and other governments, there’s less to complain about and therefore less to dampen a countries relationship.

Political Climate

On one hand the "Democratic Peace Theory," stipulates that democracies don’t go to war with one another. Consequently, the more democratic any country is, the less likely they are to engage in hostilities with the United States. The other aspect is that people are passionate and excitable about human rights abuses, when bilateral meetings occur between an open and liberal regime and a closed/illiberal regime, the liberal regime faces pressure to bring up human rights as a Cause célèbre (Pussy Riot in Russia, Liu Xiaobo in China). These differences lead to tension and friction which serves to undermine ties-if both countries have the same world view-reporters and publics are less likely to espouse opposing view points-thus avoiding either country “on the spot.” Therefore, the areas chosen for evaluation are Political Freedom-which measures how great ones ability is to exercise their rights and avoid oppression. Civil Liberties measures how many rights and freedoms individuals have in each country, while the Freedom Status, Freedom from Corruption, Press Freedom Rank measure specific freedoms that are essential to vibrant democracy, the higher a country scores on these ranks-the less squishy any interactions between the countries become. The Political Terror Scale measures violence and physical oppression on the part of the state-if a country is murdering its citizens or is dangerous for people to travel to-it creates an obvious impediment to closer ties.

Zeitgeist

This area looks at what are the values and priorities of a country in a soft-power perspective. Does the country want “to do good” in the world, and be a productive contributing member of the global commons or do they think let someone else handle it? If the country strives to be a “team player” many more countries will want that country on their “team” as such a state would be inclined to set up scientific joint ventures, charitable missions, and would increase the likelihood of interaction in multiple more domains. Therefore, the World Giving Index, and Simon Anholt’s measurements of “Science and Technology”, “Culture”, “Prosperity and Equality”, “Health and Wellbeing,” serve as good proxies for a country’s priorities in this domain.

Financial Climate

While the above sections evaluated the political openness, financial freedom can be equally important in establishing ties and cementing relationships. Since if a country is financially impenetrable or requires excessive costs to break into, and hosts an opaque regulatory regime, few investors may be inclined to export capital to such a country, consequently limiting the scope for further ties. However, if a market is especially open and transparent, and hand of the government is neither odious nor onerous then the costs of investment will be minimal and avenues of interaction will spring in multiple venues. In this vein the subsequent variables were chosen as proxies for this position. Gini Coefficient -as a measurement of economic equality and a leveled playing field. Labor Freedom-as a measurement of worker mobility and a lack of corporate ossification, Tariff Rate-as a measurement of national economic liberality, Income Tax Rate-as a measurement of a government’s weight upon its denizens, and Tax Burden as Percent of GDP as a capturing of the “Laffer curve.”

Global Climate

This domain evaluates more of Simon Anholt’s measurements and how a country’s overall perspective of their priorities can be evaluated. Therefore Anholt’s analysis the Good Country Overall Score, International Peace and Security, World Order, Planet and Climate are included in this category.

United Nations

Arguably this-a measurement of voting correlation with the United States at the United Nations is the single most important variable for determining a country’s “closeness” or “relationship” with another country. As all types of issues are discussed at the UN, therefore a high score in this category implies that both countries see eye-to-eye, a long list of different issue areas and that both countries are willing to take the same position publicly and where it counts. While countries with a low score disagree on a multitude of different issue areas-therefore serving as a emblem of weaker ties.
