Prestige

This area measures how many Nobel prizes and Olympic games each country has attended and the number of billionaires. The idea being that a country with a high prestige score has wealth, science and sport-consequently they a proud of their accomplishments and are less likely to cower and are more likely to take responsibility in the international arena-raising the value of their friendship.

The first of Business Relations is to evaluate the climate of starting a business, the concept being that the easier it is to start a business, the more likely people will do so, thus increasing ties between both nations. Ergo, the greater the likelihood of starting a new business, the greater potential trade will be, the more frequent, leaders will meet, and the lower the likelihood that war will break out-based on the "Capitalist Peace Theory".
Notable examples of poor climates, are the Indian license Raj, the "Brazil Cost", Consequently, data that evaluates the ease of starting a business is evaluated:
Starting a Business, Dealing with Construction Permits, Getting Electricity, Registering Property, Registering Property, Getting Credit, Resolving Insolvency

Nobel Prizes

In this area, the ease of operation for corporations are evaluated. The logic being that the easier it is for multinaionals and other corporations to operate, the more likely they are to do so. Consequently, rich and powerful corporate titans will take an interest in stable relations proliferating between both countries-lest their bottom lines are negatively impacted.
Therefore, the components under consideration are:
Paying Taxes, Trading Across Border, Enforcing Contracts, Business Freedom, Corporate Tax Rate Percentage
