Prestige

This area measures how many Nobel Prizes and Olympic Medals each country has won, in addition to the number of billionaires calling each country home. The idea being that a country with a high prestige score has wealth, science and sport-consequently they a proud of their accomplishments and are less likely to cower and are more likely to take responsibility in the international arena-raising the value of their friendship.

Olympics

For the past century, the Olympics have been the premiere tool for countries to showcase their athleticism, training and national prowess. Winning a Gold Medal-or even a Medal-at the Olympics is a long sought goal for many nations, while for the Great Powers; their desire lies in dominating the medals table. For smaller nations, an Olympic Medal signifies “arrival” on the international stage, while the Great Powers see geopolitical superiority in unrivaled victory. Consequently, all nations seek to preform as best as possible. Having considered this mentality, Olympic medals serve as a good indicator of a country’s ability to follow through on its desire, how “proud” its people are (assuming that country’s citizenry derives joy from winning), and how developed a country’s logistics and organization are. If a country can follow through on its goals, it becomes a valuable partner on diplomatic or economic ventures and can be taken more seriously on its word in the international arena. If a country’s people are proud, they’re less likely to back down in a confrontation or be accepting of minimalist positions in areas of interest. If a country has the capacity to train a variety of athletes in different skills, over many years, its society can be thought of as forward thinking, logistically competent, and world class-skills that carry over into economic and security arenas.

Nobel Prizes

A Nobel Prize represents unparalleled preeminence in its represented fields, and is considered a great honor for an individual to hold, as well as for that individual’s home country. Like Olympic medals, some nations have received far more than others. For many states, being awarded their first Nobel Prize would elicit great acclaim-for Great Powers; dominating the awards is recognized as the goal. Winning a Nobel Prize usually entails sustained and dedicated investment in science and research or a rich culture and literary heritage. Consequently, winning a prize can be viewed as a multiyear effort to foster excellence and the overcoming of many logistical hurdles.

Billionaires

A country’s ability to create and retain its wealthiest, speaks volumes about the productive capacity of the country and its overall society. Aside from those who derive their wealth from a historically wealthy monarchy, most billionaires made their fortune and owe their success in some part to the country where they made it. In effect countries with a high number of billionaires-at the bare minimum, have lots of money-they additionally have individuals with significant sway, a productive economy, and a government that isn’t horribly repulsive. All aspects of latent national power.
