Areas of Analysis

8 Major domains comprise QuantPolitik's evaluation of the strength of any country's bilateral relationship with the United States. Below is a brief overview of each domain.

Bureaucratic Exchange

This domain evaluates how frequently the leaders of the United States interact with top-level personnel from each evaluated country. The more frequently US leaders interact with a certain country's leadership, and the higher the level of interaction, the higher a country's score will be in this domain. This measurement reflects the concept that close friends and allies, meet frequently and at the highest levels.

Business Relations

This domain measures how easy it is for a company to do business in any particular country. The theory being that the easier it is to business the closer the business relationship will be, thus leading to closer economic and diplomatic ties, consequently strengthening the bilateral relationship.

Country Profile

The area of analysis where core aspects, namely the size, wealth, and simmilarities of a country's identity are evaluated.

Cultural Diffusion

This area mainly evaluates population transfers, whether immigrant or tourist based, the ease at which such populations may transfer, and two proxy measurements for Americanization.

Governmental Perspective

Here is where the “freedom credentials” of each country are evaluated. Political freedom, press freedom, freedom from corruption and other areas are evaluated. Freedom isn’t held in high regard because America is “free,” rather the concept is that free societies are less likely to do things that are going to upset the other country. Countries with respect for human right create fewer problems for their citizens, citizens of other countries and other governments, there’s less to complain about and therefore less to dampen a countries relationship.

Prestige

This area measures how many Nobel Prizes and Olympic Medals each country has won, in addition to the number of billionaires calling each country home. The idea being that a country with a high prestige score has wealth, science and sport-consequently they a proud of their accomplishments and are less likely to cower and are more likely to take responsibility in the international arena-raising the value of their friendship.

Security

In this domain, America’s Security relationships are evaluated, taking into account, which countries host US Military forces, whether or not they enhance or subtract from their Security environment, their potential for aggressive confrontation and shared multilateral organization. This domain seeks to evaluate whether or not the selected country is powerful or not and if they are receptive to US military Interests. High scorers are powerful and capable nations that are receptive to US interests, while low scorers are weak countries that are less receptive to US forces.

Trade Relations

The area of analysis where a nation's receptiveness to trade and the the previous half decade's trading volume is evaluated.
