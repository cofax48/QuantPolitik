Bureaucratic Exchange

At the core of a bilateral relationship is the face-to-face interaction between each government’s Principals. The more they interact, the better the relationship they will have, the more likely they will be able to reach out to one another during a crisis, the more attuned they will be to each other’s priorities and the more both sides will aim to lessen tension and/or controversy. This domain can easily be seen as the most important variable in ascribing a numerical representation of a country’s relationship. If Country A’s leaders meet numerous times a year will Country B’s leaders, and meets their entire governing team, and both countries bestow the high honor of a State Dinner upon one another-it is not a stretch to say that both countries enjoy closer ties in comparison with Country C-who’s leaders have never met with Country A’s leaders. Therefore, when analyzing a bilateral relationship, the best place to start is with the personal relationship between heads of state, and then between the foreign minister and their peers.

Secretary of State

In this category, the meetings and interactions of the Secretary of State are analyzed and quantified. If the Secretary met with the Head of State and/or Government for any country, then that country gains 2 points for each day that such a meeting occurred. If the Secretary met with a Government Minister/Secretary, then that country gains 1 point for each day that such a meeting occurred. And if the Secretary or President attends a State Dinner or Hosts one, then that country gains 5 points. If the Secretary/President attends a multilateral institution’s event (G-20 Summit, G-7, NATO, etc.) then all participant member states gain 1 point for such an event. If the President/Secretary holds a bilateral meeting during such proceedings, the country with which the President/Secretary held the meeting gains an additional point. The logic behind this data variable is identical to that of the President.

Presidential Exchange

This area is pretty straightforward, the more often the President of the United States meets with a certain country’s leaders the more important they are to the United States. Additionally the more times they meet usually reflects common membership in multilateral organizations, thus reflecting further their similarities.

Vice Presidential Exchange

This area is also very straightforward, the more often the Vice President of the United States meets with a certain country’s leaders the more important they are to the United States. Additionally the more times they meet usually reflects common membership in multilateral organizations, thus reflecting further their similarities.
